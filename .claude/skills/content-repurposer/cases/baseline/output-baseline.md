## Newsletter Draft

**Subject: Why Most A/B Tests Fail (And How to Fix Yours)**

Hi everyone,

In this issue, we're diving into a topic that might ruffle some feathers: why most A/B tests are basically theater.

I recently came across a fascinating conversation with Priya Nair, an experimentation lead, and she shared some eye-opening insights about why most A/B testing programs don't actually work.

**The Problem: Underpowered Tests**

According to Priya, around 80% of the A/B tests she's audited were underpowered. This means they didn't have enough traffic to detect the effect the team was hoping for, even if the effect was real.

Here's a concrete example. A landing page converting at 3% needs roughly 50,000 visitors per variation to reliably detect a 10% lift. But most teams only get around 8,000 visitors a week. To do it right, they'd need three months — but nobody waits that long. Instead, they wait 10 days, see a variant up 12%, and call it a win.

The problem is that the 12% is almost entirely noise. Early in a test, the numbers swing wildly. A variant can be up 40% on day 2 and down 15% by day 9.

**The Solution: Three Best Practices**

So what separates the teams that get it right? Priya points to three things:

1. They calculate sample size before launching, not after.
2. They test bigger swings, because bigger effects are detectable with less traffic.
3. They're willing to kill tests that can't reach significance.

**Key Takeaway**

At the end of the day, you'll learn more from 8 real experiments a year than from 80 fake ones. Volume of tests is a vanity metric — decisions you can trust are the only output that matters.
