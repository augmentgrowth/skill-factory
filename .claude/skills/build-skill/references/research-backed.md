# Research-backed path

The builder wants the skill to encode what *great* looks like — not just their current
process. Research external exemplars first, then rejoin the shared spine
(baseline → draft → critique → test). The research changes what you put in the draft;
it never changes the done gate.

## Step A — Check for web access first

This path needs live web tools (search / fetch). **If the session has none, do not
fabricate sources.** Say plainly: "I don't have web access in this session, so I can't
research external best practice — let's take the describe-first path and fold in any
reference material you already have." Then run `references/describe-first.md`, inviting
the builder to paste articles, examples, or guides they trust as the research substitute.

## Step B — Scope the research question

One tight question beats a broad survey. From the builder's workflow, form:
"What separates expert-level [the specific output] from amateur work?"
Confirm the scope with the builder before searching — a skill for "cold outreach for
selling services" needs different research than "all sales communication."

## Step C — Research

Search for (multiple queries, not one):

1. **Foundational experts** — who is recognized, and what frameworks do they teach?
2. **What actually works** — case studies, real examples, patterns with evidence.
3. **Anti-patterns** — what makes output in this domain bad or generic? The tells.
4. **The WHY** — the psychology or mechanics behind the good techniques.

Go deep enough that principles emerge; surface-level research produces a
surface-level skill. Keep track of the source (author/site) for every finding you
intend to use.

## Step D — Distill into principles and anti-patterns, with citations

Turn findings into transferable rules, not a list of facts:

- Each principle states the WHY ("short beats long — readers scan; each long sentence
  measurably cuts replies") and names its source.
- Anti-patterns are explicit ("template openers signal mass-send") with their source.
- **The skill's principles must cite at least two distinct external sources** (author or
  publication names inline — e.g. "(per Berman's 3C's framework)"). If the research
  genuinely surfaced fewer than two usable sources, tell the builder and either research
  further or fall back to their own material — never pad with invented citations.

Discard anything that contradicts the builder's actual context; confirm judgment calls
with them.

## Step E — Rejoin the spine

Return to `SKILL.md` Step 2 and run the standard flow with the research in hand:

1. **Baseline capture** exactly as normal — frozen input, genuine no-skill output,
   birth commit. The research does not touch the baseline; it must stay default-Claude.
2. **Classify + split** (Step 3), **draft** (Step 4) — the distilled principles and
   anti-patterns become the core of the draft, cited inline.
3. **Self-critique, side-by-side test, done** (Steps 5–7) — unchanged. The research-backed
   skill must beat the baseline like any other; good citations don't substitute for
   winning the side-by-side.
