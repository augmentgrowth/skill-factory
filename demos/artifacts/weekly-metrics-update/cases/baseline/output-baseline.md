# Weekly Channel Update — Leadership

## Headline

Spend rose ~7% week-over-week (~$55.4K vs ~$51.0K) while conversions were roughly flat (987 vs 1,012, down 2.5%). Blended CPA moved from $50.42 to $56.15 (+11%). Effectively all of the added spend went into Google Search Nonbrand, and it did not produce proportional volume.

## What Moved

**Google Search — Nonbrand (the story of the week)**
Spend up $4.2K (+30%) for 9 more conversions (+4%). CPA went from $69.27 to $85.98 (+24%). Impressions up 28%, so we bought reach that didn't convert at prior efficiency. This single line accounts for ~$4.2K of the ~$4.4K total spend increase and is the primary driver of the blended CPA rise.

**Meta — Prospecting (soft, not spend-driven)**
Spend essentially flat (-1%), conversions down 27 (-16%), CPA $76.33 → $89.79 (+18%). Impressions also down 4%. Performance decayed on its own — worth checking for creative fatigue or auction/delivery shifts.

**Display — Remarketing (small but sharp)**
Conversions fell 12 → 4 on flat spend. CPA $73.33 → $225.00. The absolute dollars are small ($900), but a 3x CPA swing on a remarketing line usually signals a tracking, tag, or audience-size problem rather than genuine demand loss. Worth a look before drawing conclusions.

**Affiliate**
Conversions down 71 → 64 (-10%) on flat spend; CPA $33.10 → $37.50. Still one of the more efficient paid lines. Mild softness, not alarming.

## What Held Up

- **Email — Promotional:** 86 conversions (+9%) at zero media cost. Best incremental return of the week.
- **Meta — Retargeting:** 118 conversions at $27.97 CPA. Efficient and stable.
- **Google Brand:** 198 conversions at $20.81. Stable, as expected.
- **Performance Max:** 131 conversions at $73.28, essentially unchanged.
- **TikTok — Prospecting:** 21 conversions at $88.10, improved from $100.00 on slightly lower spend. Small base, but trending the right way.
- **LinkedIn — Sponsored:** 9 conversions at $233.33, improved from $292.86. Still our most expensive line by a wide margin; 9 conversions is too small a base to read much into either direction.

## Read

The efficiency decline is concentrated, not systemic. Six of ten channels held steady or improved. The blended CPA increase is mostly one deliberate decision (scaling Nonbrand) plus one genuine performance dip (Meta Prospecting).

The Nonbrand result is the one that matters for planning. A 30% spend increase returning 4% more conversions suggests we're past the efficient frontier on the current keyword/query mix — we're paying more to reach an audience with lower intent. That's a normal outcome when scaling, but it means the next dollar there is expensive.

## Caveats

A few things worth flagging before anyone acts on these numbers:

- **One week is a short window.** These are week-over-week deltas with no trailing baseline, so we can't distinguish a real trend from normal weekly variance. Meta Prospecting's 27-conversion drop and Affiliate's 7-conversion drop are both within the range that ordinary fluctuation can produce.
- **Display — Remarketing is likely a measurement issue, not a performance issue.** 4 conversions is too small a number to support the $225 CPA as a meaningful figure. I'd verify tracking before treating it as a real decline.
- **These are last-touch channel numbers.** Email's 86 conversions at $0 cost look excellent in isolation, but email largely converts demand that paid channels generated. Same caution applies to Brand Search and Meta Retargeting — low CPA on capture channels partly reflects work done upstream. Cutting the expensive top-of-funnel lines would likely degrade the cheap ones.
- **No revenue or LTV data in this export.** CPA alone can't tell us whether the Nonbrand expansion is buying worse customers or simply more expensive ones. If Nonbrand conversions carry higher order value, an $86 CPA may still be fine.

## Suggested Next Steps

1. **Nonbrand:** Pull a search-term and match-type breakdown to see where the incremental 28% of impressions came from. If it's broad-match expansion into lower-intent queries, we can trim back to the efficient core. Decide explicitly whether we're scaling for volume (accept the CPA) or optimizing for efficiency (pull back).
2. **Meta Prospecting:** Check creative fatigue metrics — frequency, CTR trend, time-since-refresh. A 16% conversion drop on flat spend and flat-ish delivery usually points to creative or audience saturation.
3. **Display Remarketing:** Verify tag firing and audience list size before treating this as a performance signal. If tracking is fine, this line's $900/week is a candidate for reallocation.
4. **Pull revenue by channel** for next week's update. We're making spend decisions on CPA alone, which can't tell us whether efficiency changes are good or bad in profit terms.
