After With the skill 143 words

We're at $56 blended against our $65 target — a good week, one real dip to work.

• The nonbrand scale-up is doing what we designed it to do: +$4.2K bought 9 more conversions at $86 CPA. That's the cost of the test, and blended still sits $9 under target. Holding the budget through next week.

• Meta prospecting is the one real problem — CPA $76→$90 on flat spend, conversions 169→142. Reads like creative fatigue on our second-largest line. Pulling frequency and CTR trend this week.

• Affiliate slipped to 64 conversions from 71 at a $37 CPA on flat spend — small enough to watch, not act on. Checking partner mix if it repeats.

• Email delivered 86 conversions at zero media cost, our strongest incremental return again.

• Display remarketing conversions fell 12→4 — tag check, not a demand signal.

The "before" is not a strawman. It's genuinely good analysis — it found the nonbrand story on its own, flagged Display as a probable tracking bug, and volunteered the last-touch attribution caveat unprompted. Nobody wrote it to lose.

That's the actual lesson. A skill doesn't make Claude smarter. It makes Claude yours. Every number in the "before" is right. It just buries the verdict, re-opens a decision you'd already made, spends a paragraph on a $900 line nobody manages, and hands leadership four things to decide. The skill didn't add intelligence — it added your target, your intent, and your format.

When it breaks

Say so. The factory writes the failure down as a test case, fixes it, re-runs that exact case to prove the fix, records the trap in Gotchas, and commits. One failure, one permanent lesson.

When you correct it

Corrections you make in a session become proposed edits, ranked by confidence. Nothing is applied without you approving it.

When you want it everywhere

Graduate it — it gets installed so it works in any folder, carrying its Gotchas and its history with it. Install after it wins, never before.

When you want to undo

"Go back to yesterday's version." That's the whole command. The rollback point exists because the factory tagged it without being asked.
